Scope & Ethics

Professional boundaries, responsibilities, and operating standards.

Purpose

This page exists to clearly define what 1440 Advisory Group does, what it does not do, and how authority, consent, and responsibility are governed. Clarity protects clients, families, partners, institutions, and outcomes.

Our role (defined)

1440 Advisory Group provides clinically aligned advisory, coordination, and continuity services for individuals, families, and organizations navigating complex, high-risk transitions.

We operate alongside licensed medical, psychiatric, and therapeutic providersâ€”not in place of them. Our work focuses on structure, decision support, coordination, accountability, and continuity across transitions.

What we do not do

  • provide medical, psychiatric, or therapeutic treatment
  • diagnose conditions or prescribe medications
  • replace licensed clinicians or facilities
  • override clinical judgment
  • operate without defined authority and consent

Relationship to licensed care

Licensed providers retain full clinical authority at all times. When clinical care is indicated, 1440 coordinates logistics, communication, and continuity while clinical decisions remain the responsibility of the licensed provider.

Authority, consent, and boundaries

  • decision-making authority is defined in writing before engagement
  • information sharing is consent-based and role-defined
  • escalation and disengagement criteria are established in advance
  • work does not proceed when authority or cooperation cannot be clarified

Privacy and confidentiality

1440 operates with HIPAA-aware workflows and alignment with applicable privacy standards, including 42 CFR Part 2 where relevant. Information is shared only with appropriate consent, on a minimum-necessary basis, and within legally permissible boundaries.

Professional conduct

  • clear scope boundaries
  • respect for licensure and institutional roles
  • disciplined communication standards
  • avoidance of dependency creation or over-promising
  • selective acceptance and right to disengage when required

This Scope & Ethics framework governs all 1440 Advisory Group engagements.
